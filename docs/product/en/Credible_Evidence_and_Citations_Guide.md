# How to Improve "Credible Evidence and Citations" in Your SEO Strategy

> Version: 1.0 | Written: November 9, 2025

---

## Preface

"Credible Evidence and Citations" measures whether your content cites reliable sources and data to support claims.

Low scores mean:
- Lack of citations from reliable sources
- Lack of data and statistics
- Content lacks persuasiveness

---

## WHY - Why Is Your Credible Evidence and Citations Score Low?

### Root Causes

**Problem 1: Lack of Citations**
- No citations from reliable sources
- Using vague phrases like "reportedly" or "allegedly"
- Result: Content isn't credible

**Problem 2: Lack of Data**
- No specific statistics
- Using vague terms like "many" or "most"
- Result: Can't convince readers

**Problem 3: Unreliable Citation Sources**
- Citing unknown websites
- Citing outdated data
- Result: Reduces content credibility

---

## HOW - How to Diagnose and Improve

### Diagnostic Method

**Step 1: Count Citations**
- Scan content and count citations
- Target: At least 5-10 citations

**Step 2: Check Citation Sources**
- Are citation sources reliable?
- Do they come from authoritative institutions?

**Step 3: Check Data**
- Does content include specific statistics?
- Is the data current?

### Improvement Strategies

#### **Layer One: Add Citations**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Identify all major claims
2. Add citations for each claim
3. Ensure citation sources are reliable

#### **Layer Two: Add Data**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Find claims that can be supported with data
2. Search for relevant statistics
3. Cite these statistics in your content

#### **Layer Three: Optimize Citation Format**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Use clear citation format
2. Provide complete citation information
3. Add hyperlinks to original sources

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How many citations should I have?

**A:** No fixed answer, but typically 5-10 citations work best.

### Q2: Which sources are most reliable?

**A:** Priority order:
1. Academic research (universities, research institutions)
2. Government agencies
3. Well-known media
4. Industry experts
