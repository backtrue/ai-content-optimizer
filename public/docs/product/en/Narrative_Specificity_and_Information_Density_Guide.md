# How to Improve "Narrative Specificity and Information Density" in Your SEO Strategy

> Version: 1.0 | Written: November 9, 2025

---

## Preface

"Narrative Specificity and Information Density" measures whether your content uses concrete language and rich information, avoiding vagueness and repetition.

Low scores mean:
- Too many vague terms
- Low information density
- Content is repetitive

---

## WHY - Why Is Your Narrative Specificity and Information Density Low?

### Root Causes

**Problem 1: Using Vague Language**
- "Many," "most," "some"
- "Might," "seems," "probably"
- Result: Readers can't understand

**Problem 2: Low Information Density**
- Lots of filler words
- Repeating the same point
- Result: Content is long but useless

**Problem 3: Lack of Specific Examples**
- No specific numbers
- No specific names
- Result: Content is abstract

---

## HOW - How to Diagnose and Improve

### Diagnostic Method

**Step 1: Scan for Vague Language**
- Find all instances of "many," "most," etc.
- Calculate percentage

**Step 2: Check Information Density**
- Calculate percentage of useful information
- Target: At least 80%

**Step 3: Check Specificity**
- Are specific numbers used?
- Are specific names used?

### Improvement Strategies

#### **Layer One: Replace Vague Language**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Find all vague terms
2. Replace with specific terms
3. Add specific numbers

#### **Layer Two: Remove Redundant Content**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Find repeated points
2. Delete or merge
3. Boost information density

#### **Layer Three: Add Specific Examples**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Add specific examples for each claim
2. Use specific numbers and names
3. Provide verifiable information

---

## WHAT - Concrete Action Plan

### Immediate Improvements (1 hour)

- [ ] Scan and replace all vague language
- [ ] Remove repeated points
- [ ] Add specific numbers and examples

### Medium-term Improvements (1 week)

- [ ] Boost information density to 80%+
- [ ] Add more specific examples
- [ ] Optimize narrative style

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How do I replace vague language?

**A:** Examples:
- "Many" → "Over 50%"
- "Most" → "75% or more"
- "Some" → "Specific"

### Q2: What should information density be?

**A:** Target is 80%+. That means 80% of your text is useful information, 20% can be transition words.

Low narrative specificity and information density means "content is vague and wordy." The key to improvement is using concrete language and boosting information density.
