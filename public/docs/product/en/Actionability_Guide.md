# How to Improve "Actionability" in Your SEO Strategy

> Version: 1.0 | Written: November 9, 2025

---

## Preface

"Actionability" measures whether your content provides steps and guidance users can immediately execute.

Low scores mean:
- Your content is too theoretical, lacking practical steps
- No clear action plan
- Users don't know what to do after reading

---

## WHY - Why Is Your Actionability Low?

### Root Causes

**Problem 1: Content Is Too Theoretical**
- Only explains "why," not "how"
- Lacks specific steps and guidance
- Result: Users can't execute

**Problem 2: No Clear Action Steps**
- Steps are unclear or incomplete
- Missing prerequisites
- Result: Users are confused during execution

**Problem 3: Lack of Checklists or Tools**
- No checklists provided
- No templates or tools
- Result: Users struggle to follow along

---

## HOW - How to Diagnose and Improve

### Diagnostic Method

**Step 1: Check for Clear Steps**
- Does your content include "Step 1," "Step 2," etc.?
- Is each step clear and specific?

**Step 2: Check for Prerequisites**
- Do you explain what's needed before starting?
- Do you list required tools or resources?

**Step 3: Check for Checklists**
- Do you provide a checklist?
- Do you provide templates or tools?

### Improvement Strategies

#### **Layer One: Add Clear Steps**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Rewrite content in "Step 1," "Step 2" format
2. Each step should include: goal, action, expected result

#### **Layer Two: Add Prerequisites and Tools**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Explain prerequisites before starting
2. List required tools or resources
3. Provide ways to obtain these resources

#### **Layer Three: Provide Checklists and Templates**

**Improvement Steps:**
1. Create a checklist
2. Provide downloadable templates
3. Provide real examples

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How many steps should I have?

**A:** No fixed answer, but typically 3-7 steps work best. Too many confuses readers, too few lacks detail.

### Q2: How can I make steps clearer?

**A:** Use:
- Numbered lists (Step 1, Step 2)
- Clear headings
- Specific action verbs ("click," "enter," "select")
